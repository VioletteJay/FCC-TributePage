Mr. Verner Panton

One of the most influential designers of the 20th century

Panton's life story :

  • 1926 - Born in Gamtofte, Denmark.
  • 1947 - He studied architecture in the Royal Academy of Fine Arts of Copenhagen, where he asked himself about the role of colors in design.
  • 1953 - Panton bought a Volkswagen Combi, set up his drawing office and set off on long journeys across Europe.
  • 1955 - He set up his own agency, where his first chairs were made by the famous furniture manufacturer Fritz Hansen.
  • 1960 - Creation of the iconic Panton chair, which catapulted him to stardom. It was the world's first elastically suspended plastic chair.
  • 1963 - Panton moves to Basel, where he gets in touch with Vitra to develop the Panton chair.
  • 1968 - He designed the Flowerpot suspension, which has since been copied many times over, due to its worldwide success.
  • 1984 - During the renovation of the Circus building in Copenhagen, Verner Panton advised on color and lighting.
  • 1998 - Shortly before his death, he demonstrated his excellent mastery of color in an exhibition he had designed himself at the Trapholt Museum in Kolding.
  • 1998 - Verner Panton died in Copenhagen at the age of 72.
